Example 01

Construction Project Admin Agent

A beam-duct conflict surfaces on an $18.4M medical center expansion. Information is scattered across four email threads, a field report, coordination drawings, and meeting minutes. The PM needs a formal RFI drafted — fast.

The Scenario

A 2-inch vertical clearance conflict is discovered between a structural beam and a mechanical supply duct in the Level 1 mechanical room. Multiple parties are involved — the owner, architect, GC, and mechanical subcontractor — each with different pieces of the puzzle spread across different communications.

What Goes In

  • 4-email thread — Owner, architect, GC, and sub discussing the conflict over 3 days
  • Sub field report — Mechanical contractor's field observations with 3 resolution options and cost estimates
  • Meeting minutes — Excerpt from prior OAC meeting where the issue was first mentioned
  • Field photos — Images showing the beam-duct conflict at grid B-3/B-4
  • Coordination drawings — MEP markup showing the duct routing conflict
// Agent ingesting the mess

> Reading email-thread.md (4 emails, 3 parties)
> Reading sub-field-report.md (8 observations)
> Reading meeting-minutes-excerpt.md
> Reading field-photo-1.png, field-photo-2.png
> Reading coordination-drawing.png

Context assembled: 6 files, 4 parties,
12 drawing refs, 3 spec sections

What Comes Out

In under 30 seconds, the agent produces a complete package — all formatted, referenced, and ready for the PM to review before submission.

  • Issue Classification — Field Conflict identified at 95% confidence with sub-issues flagged (RFI needed, schedule concern, coordination gap)
  • Draft RFI-042 — Complete with subject, description, drawing references (S-301, M-301), spec sections (23 07 13), 3 resolution options from the sub, schedule impact statement, and response deadline
  • Action Item Log — 6 tasks extracted with owners, due dates, priorities, and dependencies across 4 responsible parties
  • Project Log Entry — Archival record with issue summary, parties, referenced documents, and current status
  • Risk Flags — 5-7 day schedule delay if unresolved by deadline; verbal approval from architect needs written confirmation

Example 02

Invoice-to-Cash Agent

A site grading job is complete — but there were scope changes, a verbal-only change order, and the customer needs an invoice by their AP closing date. Meanwhile, another customer's invoice is 47 days overdue.

The Scenario

Greenfield Development ordered Phase 4 site grading ($18,250 quoted). The field crew completed the work but installed 200 LF of extra silt fence (verbal approval only) and performed 4 SWPPP inspections instead of the 3 quoted (county requirement). The customer needs the invoice for their April 10 AP closing. Meanwhile, Prestige Commercial has Invoice #1087 unpaid for 47 days.

What Goes In

  • Original quote — 8 line items with unit prices, quantities, and totals for the site grading scope
  • Work completion email — From the field foreman detailing what was actually done, including extras
  • Customer email — Requesting the invoice by April 10 AP close, mentioning future Phase 5 work
  • Overdue invoice — Prestige Commercial, Invoice #1087, $8,450, now 47 days past due
// Agent ingesting billing data

> Reading original-quote.md (8 line items)
> Reading work-completion-email.md
> Reading customer-email.md
> Reading overdue-invoice.md (47 days past due)

Context assembled: quote, actuals,
2 change orders, 1 overdue account

What Comes Out

The agent produces the full billing package — reconciliation, invoice, email draft, overdue reminders, and an updated AR dashboard.

  • Billing Reconciliation — Every quote line item compared against actual work: completed as quoted, modified, or added. Change orders itemized with approval type flagged
  • Invoice #1092 — Professional invoice with original scope, change order section, deposit applied, total due ($12,375), payment terms, and bank details. Generated as JSON, Markdown, and PDF
  • Send Email — Customer-friendly email with invoice attached, amount due, due date highlighted, and a note about their Phase 5 quote request
  • Overdue Reminders — Three-tier escalation for Prestige Commercial: friendly (7-day), firm (14-day), and final notice (30+ day) with late fee calculations
  • AR Dashboard — Total outstanding, aging buckets, customer risk scores, and follow-up schedule
  • Risk Flags — Verbal-only silt fence approval needs written CO; Prestige account approaching collections threshold
